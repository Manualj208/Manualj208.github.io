Everything you need to know about Manual J, D & S calculations

Manual J is a calculation that determines the exact heating and cooling load your home needs. It's a detailed report that analyzes your home's size, construction, location, climate, and other factors to determine the correct HVAC system capacity in BTUs. This is the foundation of proper HVAC system sizing.

Manual D is the ductwork design standard. After determining your home's heating and cooling load with Manual J, Manual D specifies how to properly design and size the ductwork to distribute that conditioned air throughout your home. Good ductwork design is critical for comfort and efficiency.

Manual S is the equipment selection standard. Once you know your load (Manual J) and ductwork design (Manual D), Manual S helps you choose the right specific equipment—the correct tonnage, SEER rating, compressor type, and air handler that match your system's needs.

Manual J, D, and S work together as a complete system design. The load calculation (J) tells you what capacity you need. The ductwork design (D) tells you how to distribute it. The equipment selection (S) tells you what to install. Without all three, you're missing critical pieces that affect comfort, efficiency, and code compliance.
